# Value Proposition Canvas

## Australian Child Support Calculator (CSC)

**Last Updated:** December 28, 2024  
**Current Status:** Phase 2 - Ready for lawyer outreach  
**Website:** auschildsupport.com

---

## Overview

This Value Proposition Canvas maps our product's value proposition to two distinct customer segments:
1. **Family Law Lawyers** (primary paying customers - B2B)
2. **Separating Parents** (end users who generate leads - B2C)

---

# CUSTOMER SEGMENT 1: FAMILY LAW LAWYERS

## Customer Profile

### Customer Jobs
**Functional Jobs:**
- Acquire new family law clients consistently
- Pre-screen potential clients for case viability
- Fill appointment calendar with qualified consultations
- Generate revenue from family law matters
- Assess case complexity before initial consultation
- Manage cost of client acquisition efficiently

**Social Jobs:**
- Build professional reputation in family law community
- Be seen as accessible and helpful to potential clients
- Maintain high conversion rates (consultations to retained clients)
- Appear tech-savvy and modern to digital-native clients

**Emotional Jobs:**
- Feel confident leads are worth pursuing
- Reduce stress about lead quality and wasted time
- Feel secure about data privacy and compliance
- Trust that marketing spend is generating ROI

**Supporting Jobs:**
- Track which marketing channels work best
- Manage lead follow-up and conversion process
- Invoice and collect payment from clients
- Educate clients about realistic case outcomes

### Customer Pains
**Undesired Outcomes:**
- Paying $200-300 per click for Google Ads with low conversion
- Spending consultation time on unqualified prospects
- Parents with unrealistic expectations about outcomes
- Low-value cases that don't justify legal fees
- Missing out on high-value cases to competitors

**Obstacles:**
- Difficult to differentiate from other family lawyers
- Hard to assess case value before consultation
- Time-consuming client education during billable hours
- Unpredictable lead flow month-to-month
- High upfront costs for marketing (retainers, monthly fees)

**Risks:**
- Data privacy breaches (Privacy Act 1988 violations)
- Leads contacting competitors first
- Wasting time on tire-kickers
- Payment disputes over lead quality
- Compliance issues with lead generation practices

**Undesired Situations:**
- Empty consultation calendar with no pipeline
- Feast-or-famine client acquisition
- Losing referrals to larger firms with better marketing
- Spending more on acquisition than case is worth

**Severity:**
- **Critical:** Inconsistent revenue due to unpredictable lead flow
- **High:** Wasting time on low-value consultations (opportunity cost)
- **High:** Overpaying for unqualified leads via Google Ads
- **Medium:** Missing high-value cases that go to competitors
- **Medium:** Privacy compliance risks

### Customer Gains
**Required Gains:**
- Consistent flow of qualified leads
- Pre-screened clients who know they need help
- Complete case information before first contact
- Affordable lead cost that ensures positive ROI
- Privacy Act 1988 compliant lead generation

**Expected Gains:**
- Leads contact them quickly (within 24-48 hours)
- Parents already understand their situation
- High-value cases (worth $3,000-15,000+ in fees)
- Professional, organized lead handover process
- Clear pricing with no surprises

**Desired Gains:**
- Higher conversion rates than Google Ads
- Ability to review lead before committing
- Only pay for leads they want to pursue
- Full calculation history to assess case complexity
- Parents who already trust the referral source

**Unexpected Gains:**
- Leads include Change of Assessment reasons (saves research time)
- Parents pre-educated about child support calculations
- Database-backed lead tracking for follow-up
- Relationship with reliable lead generation partner
- Word-of-mouth referrals to other lawyers

**Importance:**
- **Critical:** Cost-effective lead acquisition ($50 vs $200-300)
- **Critical:** Pre-qualified leads (parent already knows they need help)
- **High:** Complete information (calculation history, CoA reasons)
- **High:** Low risk (review lead before purchase via teaser email)
- **Medium:** Professional appearance and privacy compliance

**Compliance & Trust:**

- Consistent lead flow (as traffic grows) ✅

- Database ensures no leads lost or forgotten

# CUSTOMER SEGMENT 2: SEPARATING PARENTS

## Customer Profile

### Customer Jobs
**Functional Jobs:**
- Calculate child support obligations or entitlements
- Understand if current arrangement is fair
- Prepare for mediation or court proceedings
- Budget for post-separation finances
- Determine if legal help is needed
- Gather evidence for Change of Assessment application

**Social Jobs:**
- Be seen as fair and reasonable by ex-partner
- Demonstrate to family/friends they're being responsible
- Ensure children's needs are prioritized
- Avoid appearing cheap or vindictive

**Emotional Jobs:**
- Feel confident in financial decisions
- Reduce anxiety about separation process
- Gain clarity about uncertain future
- Feel empowered with knowledge
- Avoid feeling overwhelmed by legal complexity

**Supporting Jobs:**
- Print or save calculations for mediator/lawyer
- Compare different scenarios (custody changes, income changes)
- Research Change of Assessment grounds
- Understand what happens next in process

### Customer Pains
**Undesired Outcomes:**
- Paying or receiving wrong amount of support
- Making mistakes that harm children financially
- Losing money to expensive lawyers for simple cases
- Being taken advantage of by ex-partner
- Not knowing if their situation is "normal"

**Obstacles:**
- Child support calculations are complex
- Don't know if they need a lawyer or can handle alone
- Can't afford expensive legal consultations for basic questions
- Services Australia website is confusing
- Scared of making costly mistakes

**Risks:**
- Making wrong financial decisions
- Agreeing to unfair arrangement out of ignorance
- Wasting money on unnecessary legal help
- Missing deadline for court or assessment
- Privacy concerns with sharing personal financial data

**Undesired Situations:**
- Feeling stupid or overwhelmed
- Having to talk to ex-partner without preparation
- Going to mediation without knowing numbers
- Discovering they've been underpaid for years
- Realizing situation is complex but can't afford lawyer

**Severity:**
- **Critical:** Not knowing if they're being treated fairly
- **Critical:** Risk of financial harm to children
- **High:** Anxiety and stress about the unknown
- **High:** Wasting money on unnecessary legal fees
- **Medium:** Embarrassment from lack of knowledge

### Customer Gains
**Required Gains:**
- Accurate calculation based on current Australian law
- Clear explanation they can understand
- Free access (no payment required)
- Quick results (no waiting for appointments)
- Privacy (no personal data shared without consent)

**Expected Gains:**
- Simple interface (mobile-friendly)
- Explanation of how calculation works
- Printable results to share with mediator/ex
- Information about what to do next
- No signup or account creation required

**Desired Gains:**
- Comparison of different scenarios
- Identification of complex situations
- Guidance on whether legal help is needed
- Connection to trusted lawyer if needed
- Reassurance they're doing the right thing

**Unexpected Gains:**
- Change of Assessment guidance (10 grounds explained)
- Detailed complexity breakdown (not just "it's complex")
- Educational content about child support process
- Instant access 24/7 (late night after kids in bed)
- Professional tool that feels trustworthy

**Importance:**
- **Critical:** Accurate calculations (based on official formula)
- **Critical:** Free access (can't afford to pay)
- **High:** Clear explanations (avoid legal jargon)
- **High:** Identification of when legal help is needed
- **Medium:** Ability to connect with lawyer if necessary

# TWO-SIDED MARKET DYNAMICS

## How Value Propositions Reinforce Each Other

### Network Effects:
- **More parents** → More leads → More lawyer interest
- **More lawyers** → Better geographic coverage → Better parent experience
- **Quality leads** → Happy lawyers → Word-of-mouth growth
- **Accurate calculator** → Parent trust → Higher inquiry conversion

### Virtuous Cycle:
1. Parents use free calculator → Trust the tool
2. Complexity detected → Parent sees value in legal help
3. Inquiry submitted → High-quality lead for lawyer
4. Lawyer converts lead → Positive ROI
5. Lawyer refers other lawyers → Network growth
6. More lawyers → Geographic coverage improves
7. Better coverage → More parents get connected
8. More traffic → More leads → More revenue
9. More revenue → Better marketing → More traffic

### Critical Balance Points:
- **Lead quality** - Must maintain >80% satisfaction to keep lawyers
- **Traffic volume** - Need sufficient parents to feed lawyer demand
- **Geographic distribution** - Lawyers need leads in their service area
- **Response time** - Parents expect quick lawyer follow-up

# PRICING VALIDATION

## Lawyer Willingness to Pay: $50 per Lead

### Value Justification:
- **Alternative cost:** Google Ads = $200-300 per click (not even guaranteed lead)
- **Lawyer revenue:** Average family law case = $3,000-15,000
- **Conversion rate:** If 1 in 20 leads converts = $3,000 revenue from $1,000 spend = 3x ROI
- **Risk mitigation:** Teaser email approval = only pay for leads you want

### Competitive Pricing:
- Legal lead generation industry standard: $50-200 per lead
- Our pricing: $50 (low end of market)
- Quality justification: Manual review, complexity detection, full information

### Price Sensitivity:
- **Too low (<$25):** Perceived as low quality
- **Sweet spot ($50):** Affordable, high-volume purchase
- **Too high (>$100):** Requires calendar integration (confirmed booking)

# RISKS TO VALUE PROPOSITION

## Lawyer Side:
1. **Lead quality doesn't match expectations**
   - Mitigation: Manual quality control, refund policy
2. **Insufficient lead volume**
   - Mitigation: SEO focus, marketing to parents
3. **Geographic mismatch** (leads not in lawyer's area)
   - Mitigation: Track location, route appropriately
4. **Low conversion rates** (leads don't become clients)
   - Mitigation: Ensure leads are genuinely complex cases

## Parent Side:
1. **Calculator inaccuracy**
   - Mitigation: Based on official formula, annual updates
2. **Privacy concerns**
   - Mitigation: Privacy Act compliance, consent required
3. **Pressure to use lawyer** (feels like sales pitch)
   - Mitigation: Inquiry is optional, no forced signup
4. **Website trust** (looks like scam)
   - Mitigation: Professional design, privacy policy, no payment required
