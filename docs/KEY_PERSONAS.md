# Key Personas

## Child Support Calculator (CSC)

---

## Persona 1: Sarah - The Newly Separated Parent

### Demographics
- **Age**: 34
- **Location**: Suburban area
- **Occupation**: Retail manager
- **Income**: $55,000/year
- **Education**: Bachelor's degree
- **Family**: Recently separated, 2 children (ages 5 and 8)
- **Tech Savvy**: Moderate (comfortable with apps and websites)

### Background
Sarah recently separated from her partner of 10 years. She has primary custody of their two children and is navigating the separation process while trying to maintain stability for her kids. She's concerned about finances and wants to ensure she receives fair child support while avoiding expensive legal fees if possible.

### Goals
- Understand how much child support she should receive
- Prepare for mediation discussions with her ex-partner
- Make informed decisions about whether she needs a lawyer
- Create a realistic budget for her new living situation
- Ensure her children's needs are adequately covered

### Pain Points
- Overwhelmed by the separation process
- Worried about legal costs
- Uncertain about her financial rights and obligations
- Stressed about having difficult conversations with her ex
- Limited time due to work and parenting responsibilities
- Concerned about making mistakes that could affect her children

### Behaviors
- Searches Google late at night after kids are in bed
- Reads parenting forums and support groups
- Compares information from multiple sources
- Looks for clear, step-by-step guidance
- Values transparency and honesty

### Needs from CSC
- Simple, easy-to-understand calculator
- Clear explanations of how calculations work
- Printable results to share with mediator or ex-partner
- Information about next steps in the process
- Reassurance that the calculations are accurate and legal
- Option to connect with a lawyer if needed

### Quote
"I just want to make sure my kids are taken care of. I don't know what I'm entitled to, and I can't afford to get this wrong."

## Persona 2: Marcus - The Non-Custodial Father

### Demographics
- **Age**: 41
- **Location**: Urban area
- **Occupation**: IT consultant
- **Income**: $95,000/year
- **Education**: Master's degree
- **Family**: Divorced, 1 child (age 12), shares custody
- **Tech Savvy**: High (works in technology)

### Background
Marcus divorced three years ago and shares custody of his daughter. He wants to ensure he's paying the correct amount of child support and is considering requesting a modification due to changes in his income and custody arrangement. He's analytical and wants to understand the details.

### Goals
- Verify current child support payments are correct
- Calculate potential changes if custody arrangement changes
- Understand how income changes affect obligations
- Plan financially for the future
- Maintain a fair and transparent relationship with his ex

### Pain Points
- Suspects current arrangement may not reflect actual circumstances
- Wants to modify support but doesn't want to appear cheap
- Concerned about legal costs for minor adjustments
- Needs to understand complex scenarios (overtime, bonuses, shared custody)
- Wants data to support conversations with ex-partner

### Behaviors
- Researches thoroughly before making decisions
- Keeps detailed financial records
- Prefers data-driven solutions
- Values accuracy and precision
- Willing to pay for quality service if value is clear

### Needs from CSC
- Detailed breakdown of calculation methodology
- Ability to model different scenarios
- Professional-looking reports to share with ex or lawyer
- Technical accuracy and up-to-date legal information
- Option to save and compare different calculations

### Quote
"I want to do right by my daughter, but I also need to make sure the numbers actually make sense for our situation."

## Persona 3: Jennifer - The Family Lawyer

### Demographics
- **Age**: 38
- **Location**: Regional city
- **Occupation**: Family law attorney
- **Income**: $120,000/year
- **Education**: Law degree
- **Practice**: Small firm, 15+ years experience
- **Tech Savvy**: Moderate (uses legal software, comfortable with online tools)

### Background
Jennifer runs a small family law practice and handles numerous child support cases. She's looking for tools to help her clients understand their situations before initial consultations and to quickly verify calculations during case preparation.

### Goals
- Pre-screen potential clients
- Educate clients about realistic expectations
- Save time on basic calculations
- Provide quick estimates during consultations
- Refer clients to reliable resources
- Streamline case preparation

### Pain Points
- Spends too much time on basic calculation questions
- Clients come with unrealistic expectations
- Needs to justify fees for straightforward cases
- Wants to focus on complex legal strategy, not basic math
- Limited time for client education

### Behaviors
- Refers clients to trusted resources
- Values professional reputation
- Needs tools that are legally accurate
- Appreciates time-saving solutions
- Willing to recommend tools that help clients
- May refer overflow or out-of-area cases

### Needs from CSC
- Accurate calculations aligned with legal standards
- Professional presentation for client sharing
- Easy referral process for clients who need full representation
- Disclaimer that protects against liability
- Potential for two-way referral relationship

### Quote
"If clients can understand the basics before they come to me, we can spend our time on what really matters - the strategy and negotiation."

## Persona 4: Alex - The Mediator

### Demographics
- **Age**: 52
- **Location**: Metropolitan area
- **Occupation**: Family mediator
- **Income**: $85,000/year
- **Education**: Social work background, mediation certification
- **Experience**: 20 years in family services
- **Tech Savvy**: Moderate

### Background
Alex helps separating couples reach agreements without going to court. They need neutral, transparent tools to facilitate discussions and help couples understand the financial realities of child support.

### Goals
- Facilitate productive conversations
- Provide neutral, objective information
- Help couples reach fair agreements
- Reduce conflict and emotional tension
- Ensure agreements are realistic and sustainable

### Pain Points
- Couples come with wildly different expectations
- Needs trusted, neutral tools to establish common ground
- Arguments over calculations derail mediation sessions
- Limited tools designed specifically for mediation context
- Needs to remain impartial while providing accurate information

### Behaviors
- Uses tools during mediation sessions
- Shares resources with clients between sessions
- Values transparency and neutrality
- Focuses on collaborative solutions
- Educates clients about process

### Needs from CSC
- Neutral, unbiased presentation
- Clear methodology that both parties can trust
- Ability to model different scenarios in real-time
- Professional output to include in mediation agreements
- Educational materials about child support

### Quote
"When both parties can see the same numbers and understand how they're calculated, we can move past the arguing and focus on what's best for the children."

### Demographics
- **Age**: 26
- **Location**: Small town
- **Occupation**: Administrative assistant
- **Income**: $38,000/year
- **Education**: Some college
- **Family**: Pregnant, not in relationship with baby's father
- **Tech Savvy**: High (digital native, uses smartphone for everything)

### Background
Priya is pregnant and not in a relationship with the baby's father. They've agreed he'll provide financial support, but neither knows how much is appropriate. She's trying to plan her budget and understand what to expect before the baby arrives.

### Goals
- Understand potential child support amount
- Plan budget for baby expenses
- Have informed conversation with baby's father
- Avoid future legal conflicts
- Ensure fair arrangement without animosity

### Pain Points
- Never dealt with legal/financial matters like this before
- Scared and uncertain about the future
- Doesn't know where to start
- Limited budget for legal help
- Wants to avoid conflict but protect her rights

### Behaviors
- Uses smartphone for all online activities
- Watches YouTube tutorials
- Seeks advice from friends and online communities
- Values simple, visual explanations
- Concerned about costs

### Needs from CSC
- Mobile-friendly interface
- Very simple, step-by-step process
- Clear explanations in plain language
- Free or low-cost basic service
- Guidance on what to do next
- Emotional reassurance and support

### Quote
"I'm trying to figure this all out before the baby comes. I don't even know what questions to ask."
